Abstract

Dexterous grasping faces challenges of high degrees of freedom (DOFs) and complex grasping contacts. In this paper, we propose a novel solution with two-stage pipeline of grasp pose synthesis and motion planning which leverages contact, temporal and grasp pose priors to address the challenges. In the first stage, a novel two-step factorization with intermediate contact maps is proposed to address the high non-linearity mapping of objects to poses. Also, a novel latent-diffusion-based contact map generative method is designed to avoid the learning difficulty in the high-dimensional object point cloud space. In the second stage, a novel trajectory optimization method incorporating the temporal and joint synergy is proposed: 1) a novel temporal parametric mapping function is proposed to map the pose parameters at trajectory steps to variables controlling the joint moving tempo; 2) a differentiable neural poser is proposed that employs a neural network to learn grasp pose prior by encoding the hand poses into a low-embedding latent space. Optimization with temporal parametric mapping and in the latent space via the differential neural poser reduces the number of parameters from thousands to dozens and help to avoid the uncanny poses during the motion planning. Lastly, with the guidance of generated contact maps, penetration-aware optimization is proposed for local pose refinement, and a joint optimization strategy is proposed to mitigate the sub-optimal issue incurred by the multi-stage pipeline. Extensive experiments on three datasets demonstrate a significant improvement over existing methods. A large-scale 4D grasp motion dataset is built with our method.
